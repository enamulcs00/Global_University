Study In Canada

Canada is another popular destination of study for International students. Canada has been welcoming international students in every city. With its range of landscapes, glacial peaks, icefields, green vegetation and massive mountains, there is a long list of Canadian scenic wonders for visitors. Whilst in Canada, most visited tourist attractions are the Niagara Falls, the 135 km long Okanagan Lake with its Ogopogo lake monster, the Glacier National Park, the Banff National Park and the Canadian Rockies.
Education system in Canada
The higher education system in Canada is not very different from the American education system, with a little influence form Britain. There are mainly three types of institutions.
  • Public Universities: Public universities in Canada are run by the provincial, territorial or federal government funding besides receiving the tuition fees from students.
  • Private Universities:Private universities in Canada are mostly funded by donations and research grants. These universities do not receive funding from the government bodies.
  • Liberal Arts Colleges: Liberal arts colleges in Canada are similar to those in the USA. These are either public or private colleges with a primary emphasis on undergraduate courses in liberal arts.
Canada as a destination of study
The United Nations consistently ranks Canada as one of the best places in the world to live, given its low crime rate. Montreal and Toronto are two large cities that has a lot to offer for International students. Toronto, an ethnically diverse city is also a major centre of higher learning and research with many well-known institutions such as University of Toronto. The City of Saints, Montreal is one that defies all expectation with its mix of North American & European swagger and cutting-edge culture. There are also walk-safe programmes, where volunteers assist people in getting to public transportation or homes during late hours at night.
Study Options in Canada
Canadian institutions offer three major intakes, Fall, Winter and Summer. The Fall intake is the primary intake in most colleges which starts from September whilst a few colleges offer a Winter intake which starts from January. International students are advised to apply as early as possible as scholarships and admissions get more competitive closer to the deadline. An application deadline would be 6 to 9 months before the session starts and it can vary depending on each department and the subject of choice though deadlines are not flexible, except for diploma programmes, some institutions might accept students if places are still available. Students can choose from a range of study programmes
  • Certificate or diploma (one or two years)
  • Advanced diploma (two or three years)
  • Undergraduate degree (three or four years)
  • Postgraduate degree (two years)
  • PhD (four or five years with dissertation)
    • Popular courses at the undergraduate levels are engineering and administrative studies and at the postgraduate level are Management, Finance, MBA and LLB degrees.
    Cost of studying in Canada
    The cost of studying in Canada is relatively cheaper compared to the other popular destinations. The tuition fees vary according to the institutions. The average tuition fee is around CAD 10,000 0 40,000 per year. Living expenses will depend on the location and the type of accommodation, i.e. private, homestay or campus accommodation. On average, students pay CAD 6,000 – 8000 per year for their accommodation. Health insurance and transport costs will be an additional cost. International students can work part-time for 20 hours per week. They are permitted to work on campus for the first 6 months and then off campus post this period. Some universities with more intensive courses only allow their students to work for 12 hours per week.
    Scholarships for international students
    Government and non-government scholarships are available for international students in Canada. These include:
    Government scholarships
  • Canada graduate scholarships
  • NSERC postgraduate scholarships
    • Non – Government scholarships
  • Canadian Rhodes Scholars Foundation scholarship
  • Trudeau scholarships and fellowships
    • In addition to this, there are institution specific scholarships for international students.
    Benefits of studying in Canada
  • There are several options to choose from big or small universities and small – town or city universities.
  • Students can enjoy an active and varied lifestyle in the beautiful country, along with great health care benefits.
  • Students will have incredible opportunities to meet like-minded people and gain valuable international student experience through student clubs and organisations.
