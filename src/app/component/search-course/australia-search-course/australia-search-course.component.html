Study In Australia

Australia is the third popular study destination for international students after the USA and the UK. Australia is home to the world’s eight most popular universities. Its tertiary education comprises of vocational colleges and universities, with 22,000 courses on offer for foreign students.
Education system in Australia
The public vocation education system in Australia is called Technical and Further Education (TAFE). TAFE institutes are usually funded by the government and offer qualifications like certificates and diplomas, though TAFE schools offer undergraduate programmes as well. Some universities accept the credits earned at vocational schools like TAFE and in such cases, you can transfer your credits towards an advanced degree. Apart from TAFE, vocational qualifications could also be obtained at private vocational institutes, such as Registered Training Organisations (RTO) in Australia.

Vocational colleges are for cultivating adult learning experiences that provide certificates and diploma programmes. These schools mostly provide courses that are work-oriented and help an individual to find a source of income immediately upon completion of the course.

Australian university system comprises of both public and private universities. There are 37 public and 3 private universities. The higher education providers in Australia are either self-accrediting or non-self-accrediting or universities. The syllabus of each university is designed by the university themselves and not by any government body. Undergraduate degree courses last from 3 to 4 years, depending on if it’s a general or professional degree. Postgraduate degree programmes are categorised into Postgraduate by research which lasts from 1-2 years of full-time study and Postgraduate extended programme that lasts for 3-4 years of full-time study. Students studying in Australia can easily transfer credits and shift between study levels and institutions as per the guidelines mentioned by the Australian Qualifications Framework (AQF), a national policy followed in the Australian education and training. The AQF standards comprises of different levels, starting from 1 to 10 which includes certificate for doctoral courses.
Australia as a destination of study
Australia attracts almost 300,000 international students to its major cities and towns. According to official reports, some of the most popular Australian cities amongst international students include Melbourne, Adelaide, Perth, Brisbane and Sydney. You will lean a lot from the native people in Australia and understand different cultures. Since most of the international student population consists of students from all over the world, this is the perfect opportunity for a student to explore and meet people from all walks of life. Besides mixing with people, you will have the opportunities to come face-to-face with the “killer animals” like crocodiles, snakes and spiders. From Architectural wonders such as the iconic Sydney Opera house to the Great Barrier Reef, Australia has spectacular places to visit. The country is also a great place to explore and to get absorbed not the wildlife that has a lot to offer.

Things to look for while studying in Australia
  • People – The people in Australia are warm and welcoming. The rich indigenous community is a big part of the Australian culture and ethnically diverse societies. The country is home to migrants from over 200 countries, including the UK, China, India and New Zealand, making it a vibrant mx of cultures and lives.
  • Climate – It might take some time getting used to the climate in Australia because it varies from one place to another. The climate that you experience in the northern section may not be the case when you move towards the south.
  • Accommodation – Most students in Australia prefer off-campus accommodation. The transport system is very efficient and quick, making it easier for student to commute everyday to their universities from their location of stay. University campuses do have on-campus accommodation for international students.
  • Food and drink - There is plenty of options available from native fruits and vegetables to seafood, freshly brewed coffee to ginger beer.
  • City life – Some of the best cosmopolitan cities include Sydney and Melbourne. Most universities are in urban or sub-urban areas, so a variety of city life comes with it. You will be able enjoy recreational activities during your stay. There are several other spectacular places to visit which include the Great Barrier Reef and beaches. Every city is well connected by roads and rails and commuting from one place to another is very easy on a limited budget. Cities offer their own travel cards such a Myki card in Melbourne, Opal card in Sydney, Smart Rider card in Perth and Green card in Tasmania.
Things to do while studying in Australia
  • Relaxing on beaches during weekends is very common as most cities are near to the coasts. Most locals know how to surf and that’s a favourite pastime for many.
  • Australia has some of the spectacular places which are a must see for anyone visiting the country. There are also plenty of night clubs and pubs in every city. Adelaide is the wine capital of Australia.
  • Australia is home to several unusual and numerous species of reptiles, birds and other animals.
Study Options in Australia
The number of international students in Australia is growing every year. The most preferred courses amongst international students are Business, Tourism, Hospitality Management, MBA, Accounting, Healthcare and IT. An international student is expected to take up a course registered under CRICOS. If they intend to study in Australia. CRICOS indicated that the course and institution that is chosen meets the high standard of the Australian Education system. Australian universities offer a range of learning options such as associate degrees, undergraduate degrees, postgraduate degrees and PhD’s to international students. 40% of the international student population are engaged in higher education programmes whilst 18% in English language programmes.
Cost of studying in Australia
An undergraduate degree could cost AUD 10,000 – 30,000 and postgraduate and PhD degree could cost AUD 20,000 – 40,000. The average living cost in Australia has been estimated to be AUD 18,000 per year. Accommodation costs could be AUD 440 per week and transport and groceries could cost AUD 15 – AUD 280 per week. Whether your interest lies in nature, technology, art, culture, adventure, food, wine or world class academia, Australia has something for you and is a country of endless opportunities.
Scholarships for International students
Scholarships are offered to international students are purely based on merit. In some cases, scholarships cover both, tuition and living expenses. Apart from some universities and colleges offering scholarships, there are several government sources offering several scholarships or financial aid for international students. Some of the popular scholarships for international students are
  • Australia Awards – These scholarships are open to students looking for undergraduate or postgraduate programmes and TAFE certificates at some of the Australian institutions. The Australia Awards Scholarships cover full tuition fees return air fare and living expenses and student health cover.
  • Postgraduate scholarships – Students seeking admissions to postgraduate programmes at Australian institutions can apply for Endeavour Postgraduate Scholarship Awards or International Postgraduate Research Scholarships (IPRS)
  • Australian University Scholarships – Some of the popular Australian universities make it easier for international students to meet their tuition fees by supporting them with university specific scholarships
Benefits of studying in Australia
Australia is comparatively affordable, and the scholarships provided to foreign students make it easier for them to fund their living and tuition costs. International students can work part time (20 hours per week) during term time and full-time (up to 40 hours per week) during holidays. Australia offers post study work visas for international students which could be of the duration 18 months to 4 years.
